Their Stories

Yunhan Zhang
Age 29
Washington D.C.

“I don’t know what to say anymore, this type of hate crime happened to us again… I got sprayed on the face, arm, cloth. Some got in my mouth. I was choking badly when I called 911. I’m not injured. However, the burning has not stopped after hours.”

Noriko Nasu
Seattle

“I do definitely believe it was a hate crime, the suspect came to Chinatown with a weapon in his pocket to look for victims. When he found me, as you can see in the video, he didn’t strike my boyfriend even though my boyfriend was between me and the suspect. He deliberately went around Michael to hit me first.”

Denny Kim
Age 27
Los Angeles

“Throughout my career, I experienced a lot of micro-aggressions because of my race. I never felt like I fit in. I never felt like I belonged.”

Subhakar Khadka
San Francisco

“She started taunting me, cursing me, questioning me about being a man. The ladies were even talking about shooting me, calling their cousins to shoot me. They were making fun of my race.”

Lai
Miami

“It’s demoralizing. It’s like they view you as subhuman. Like you don’t belong, but I do belong. Diversity is what makes this country so strong, so for someone to say something like that it’s honestly so heartbreaking.”

Hanna L
Los Angeles

“I’m already prepared mentally for this kind of situation. It’s not the first time it happened to Asians. Someday it has to come to an end. Keeping silent is not the way to deal with it.”

Sophia Chang
Age 28
Newport Beach

“I feel so fortunate that I have a platform where I am able to share my experience and shine a light on racism against Asians. I know I am just one person, but I hope shedding a light on this encourages anyone who deals with racial harassment to SPEAK UP.”

on
March 8, 2021

My 11-year-old daughter came home crying from school repeatedly because children in her class said they hoped COVID would go back to China and kill all the Chinese people. They also said Chinese people are dirty, lying cheaters who can't be trusted. I reported it to the school administration and never even got a response to my email asking for support. We have never felt more invisible.

Anonymous
Submitted
on
May 11, 2021

It was around 5:00 pm on Sunday, Mother’s Day, when I entered Michaels Craft Store and noticed a man had entered after me. He did not follow me but I did see him while I was shopping. I bought what I needed and proceeded to leave the store. When I was waiting to cross into the parking lot, he then approached me — he was a man in his 30s and around 6’ tall. He began to shout, “YOU F*CKING CH*NK GO BACK TO YOUR COUNTRY!” I froze and was terrified and went to my car completely distraught and didn’t know what to do since I was alone. I am 17 years old and I am of mixed race: Japanese, Filipino, Mexican & Russian.

Submitted
on
May 14, 2021

My sister and I were walking to get in line for an Asian grocery market in Berkeley when an unmasked man from a nearby auto repair shop walked up directly to us and said, "F*ck you CCP (Chinese Communist Party), it's your fault for bringing the virus here, go back to your country!" We yelled back at him for spewing racial slurs at us and he continued to taunt us. Then he proceeded to harass all the people in line to the grocery market (majority Asian people) and went on a racist tirade, going right up into people's faces and yelling "F*ck you Chinese," along with other hateful speech.

Ways You Can Help

Below are lists of resources that Asian and non-Asian communites can utilize. These resources allow us to support Asian communities during this time of elevated violence, and offer various resources that Asian communites can take advantage of:
  • Monetary donations can be made to the various donation pages provided, including a GoFundMe and six other meaningful organizations.
  • Discrimination has major impacts on one's physical and mental well-being. If you or someone you know is struggling with mental health related to rising anti-Asian hate, explore the different AAPI mental health resources available.
  • Additional resource and awareness pages are provided to learn more about Asian American experiences, hate, and history. These additional pages also provide further opportunities to get involved through donations, organizations, and volunteering.
  • Reporting Anti-Asian discrimination is extremely important. Three websites are listed for you to report discrimination you personally experience or witness someone experience.
  • Get involved on social media! Follow the instagram pages provided to get regular updates on supporting Asian communities and fighting against hate.
